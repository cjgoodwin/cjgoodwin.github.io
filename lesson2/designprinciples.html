Contrast

Perot Museum

Perot Museum of Nature and Science

My Explanation

The Perot Museum of natural history and science uses the element of contrast to catch your eye as soon as you open their website. The black background with the bold white text, not to mention red as the accent color allows that block of the webpage to be the first thing your eye sees when you navigate to the website. They use the red block to the side of "We're Back!" and a red button to get more information about how to visit them so those will be the first things you look at. Then the hours and days in the bold white text between lets that be the next thing you look at. That is excellent web design because it effectively controls where the user's eye goes without straining it.

Alignment

Raytheon Technologies

Raytehon Website

My Explanation

Raytheon Technologies uses the alignment property to display its company values. They align the image that goes with the logo to the center and the text to state what the value is and a small catch phrase for it as justified to the left so it looks clean-cut and still has enough white space between the words and the image to catch the viewer's eye. I've noticed this is a popular technique and rightfully so since it looks clean, it draws the eye and allows the developer to help the viewer understand the importance of the content.

Law of Symmetry

Amazon

Amazon Website

My Explanation

The law of symmetry is applied here with the shopping category boxes. When you first look at the page you initially see that there is a grid of boxes. They look symmetrical on the page, meaning they are laid out at equal distances from each other all throughout the page. Regardless of the size of the product, the images inside the boxes all appear to be the same size as well so the user isn't distracted with any one particular thing. I think Amazon uses this technique because they want to sell as much as possible and don't necessarily have one particular product to promote.
